# Implementation: Method of Moments (MoM) — 2D Electrostatics

## Overview

We compute the polarizability tensor of an infinitely long dielectric prism
with arbitrary rectangular cross-section.  The problem reduces to a 2D
electrostatic surface integral equation on the perimeter of the cross-section
in the plane perpendicular to the cylinder axis.

The solver obtains the charge density induced on the surface when the object is
placed in a uniform external electric field.  The polarizability follows from
the first moment of the induced charge distribution.

## Geometry

A rectangular prism with semi-axes
\(a = l_x/2\), \(b = l_y/2\) centred at the origin in the \(xy\)-plane.
The mesh consists of line elements along the perimeter.

### Mesh generation (`gen_mesh_2d_rect`)

A uniform boundary-element mesh is generated by dividing each of the four edges
into segments.  For the uniform mesh (\(\beta = 0\)), each edge receives an
equal number of elements proportional to its length.  The element centres
\( \mathbf{r}_i \), outward unit normals \( \mathbf{n}_i \), and lengths
\( \Delta s_i \) are returned, together with the total cross-sectional area
\(A = l_x l_y\).

## Integral Equation (PMCHW Formulation)

For a 2D dielectric object with relative permittivity \(\varepsilon_r\) in a
uniform applied field \(\mathbf{E}_0\), the governing integral equation on the
boundary \(S\) is the Poggio–Miller–Chang–Harrington–Wu (PMCHW) formulation
[1, 2]:

\[
\frac{\varepsilon_r + 1}{2} \sigma(\mathbf{r})
+ \frac{\varepsilon_r - 1}{2\pi}
\int_S \sigma(\mathbf{r}')
\frac{(\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{r}') \cdot \mathbf{n}(\mathbf{r})'}
        {|\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{r}'|^2}
\, dS'
= -E_n^{\rm inc}(\mathbf{r})
\]

where \(\sigma\) is the equivalent surface charge density,
\(\mathbf{n}(\mathbf{r}')\) is the outward normal at the source point, and
\(E_n^{\rm inc} = \mathbf{E}_0 \cdot \mathbf{n}\) is the normal component of the
incident field.

## Discretization (MoM)

With pulse-basis / point-matching (collocation), the integral equation
becomes the linear system

\[
\mathbf{Z} \mathbf{x} = \mathbf{y},
\qquad
Z_{ij} =
\begin{cases}
\displaystyle \frac{\varepsilon_r + 1}{2}, & i = j \\[8pt]
\displaystyle \frac{\varepsilon_r - 1}{2\pi}
\frac{(\mathbf{r}_i - \mathbf{r}_j) \cdot \mathbf{n}_j}
        {|\mathbf{r}_i - \mathbf{r}_j|^2}
\Delta s_j, & i \neq j
\end{cases}
\]

and the RHS \(y_i = -x_i\) or \(y_i = -y_i\) depending on the applied field
direction (applied along \(x\) or \(y\)).  The system is solved with a standard
dense direct solver (LU decomposition via `numpy.linalg.solve`).

The solution \(\sigma_i\) yields the polarizability components:

\[
\alpha_{xx} = -(\varepsilon_r - 1) \sum_i \sigma_i^{(x)} n_{i,x} \Delta s_i,
\qquad
\alpha_{yy} = -(\varepsilon_r - 1) \sum_i \sigma_i^{(y)} n_{i,y} \Delta s_i
\]

where \(\sigma^{(x)}\) and \(\sigma^{(y)}\) are the solutions for applied
fields along \(x\) and \(y\), respectively.  Off-diagonal components vanish
by symmetry.  The polarizability is normalized per unit length in the
\(z\)-direction.

## Acceleration

| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Direct LU | Dense \(n \times n\) matrix, Gaussian elimination. Suitable for \(n \lesssim 2000\). |

No iterative or hierarchical matrix methods are used for the 2D solver, as the
problem size remains modest (maximum perimeter elements ~400 for our shape
grid).

## Verification

*   Symmetry: for a square cross-section, \(\alpha_{xx} = \alpha_{yy}\).
*   Monotonicity: \(\alpha\) increases with \(\varepsilon_r\).
*   Aspect-ratio ordering: the polarizability along the longer dimension is
    larger.
*   Regression: PEC square values agree with published MoM results to within 3–5%
    at moderate discretization (nd = 60).
